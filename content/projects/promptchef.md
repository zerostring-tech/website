## What Is PromptChef?

PromptChef is a community-driven library of AI prompts and skills. The library itself lives as an open-source GitHub project, where prompts and skills are contributed as markdown files. PromptChef wraps that project in a simple website at [promptchef.net](https://promptchef.net/) so anyone can browse and find what they need without digging through a repository.

## The Problem It Solves

Useful AI prompts and skills exist all over the internet — scattered across blog posts, tweets, gists, forums, and other people's private notes. There is no single, consistent place to find them, and no agreed structure for sharing or discovering them. Every time something genuinely useful is shared, it tends to disappear into whichever platform it was posted on.

PromptChef solves the discovery problem: one place to look, organized consistently, kept up to date by the people actually using it.

## How It Works

- **Browse** — Visit promptchef.net and navigate directly to the prompts and skills you need, without searching across scattered sources.
- **Contribute** — PromptChef is open source on GitHub. Anyone can contribute new prompts or skills as markdown files.
- **Attribution** — If a contributed prompt or skill originates from another platform or creator, contributors are required to credit the original source or URL.

## Why It's Useful

- **One destination, not a scavenger hunt.** Instead of searching across dozens of platforms, users go to one place built specifically for this.
- **Community-vetted, not anonymous copy-paste.** Content is contributed and reviewed openly on GitHub rather than passed around with no record of origin.
- **Properly attributed.** Required sourcing means credit goes to the original creators, not whoever reposted it last.
- **Open by design.** Anyone can contribute, so the library grows with the community instead of being limited to what one team can produce.

## The Bigger Picture

As AI prompts and skills become a normal part of more people's workflows, the gap isn't a lack of good prompts — it's that they're scattered, unattributed, and hard to find again when you need them. PromptChef aims to be the commons for that: a well-organized, properly credited, community-maintained library that gets more useful the more people contribute to it.
